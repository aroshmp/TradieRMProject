| BR1  | The prototype must include CRUD actions for managing customers, technicians, jobs, and job information and parts.                                     | Basic functionality of a CRM application.                                                                                                                                                                                                                           | Must Have       |
| BR2  | The prototype must include an API or webhook that can be integrated within an external website.                                                       | Websites typically have a Contact Us section that allows a customer to fill in the details of a job, automatically entering it into the system to be processed.                                                                                                     | Must Have       |
| BR3  | The prototype must automatically respond to client requests sent via an API or webhook to confirm the receival of the message.                        | Standard in industry to automatically respond to a request so that a client knows that the company has received their request.                                                                                                                                      | Must Have       |
| BR4  | The prototype must be able to provide a curated or generated response to a client request.                                                            | The process of responding to emails can be tedious for administrators. Having a suggestion on how to respond can help reduce administration tasks.                                                                                                                  | Must Have       |
| BR5  | The prototype must never automatically send AI generated content to a client without an administrator or technician approval.                         | LLMs are prone to prompt injection and could cause privacy or security risks. Administrators and technicians must always be involved in the process of communicating with a client to eliminate these risks.                                                        | Must Have       |
| BR6  | The prototype must be able to manage a technician's timetable of assigned jobs, adjusting bookings to include the time spent travelling between jobs. | Clients are often frustrated when a technician is late to complete a job. Having a system that ensures that technicians aren’t overbooked can help reduce this frustration.                                                                                         | Must Have       |
| BR7  | The prototype should allow administrators or clients to book when a job is going to be completed.                                                     | Clients have busy schedules and have select times when a technician can come to complete the service. Administrators should also be able to book when a job will be completed as they may talk with the client over the phone to gather information about bookings. | Should Have     |
| BR8  | The prototype could allow synchronization with a technicians personal calendar (Google Calendar, Outlook Calendar, etc)                               | The technician may have other out-of-work activities that goes along with their work life. Having the ability to look at one calendar and be able to see all jobs and extra activities in one place would allow them to organize their time effectively.            | Could Have      |
| BR9  | The prototype should be designed for both Desktop and Mobile (Web based)                                                                                          | Administrators will typically use a desktop computer for managing the system. While a technician is out on the job, they may need to write notes about what was completed for a specific job.                                                                       | Must Have       |
| BR10 | The prototype must generate an invoice which details labour and parts used on a job.                                                                  | When a job has been completed, the customer would be sent an invoice that details all of the costs from the job and instructions on how to pay.                                                                                                                     | Must Have       |
